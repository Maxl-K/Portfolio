About Me

I'm driven to bridge theory with tangible impact in technology. I strive to tackle challenges that blend software, hardware, and real-world application.

With a recent Master's in Robotics Engineering from UNSW, I specialised in the control and modelling of robotic systems, including classical control theory and modern methods including deep Neural Networks and Reinforcement Learning methods.

Over the past five years, my work has spanned from setting a Guinness World Record and writing my UG thesis with the UNSW Sunswift solar car team to leading workshops and tutoring engineering students as a teaching assistant. I've contributed to a range of projects—working as a research assistant on pedestrian detection in LIDAR point clouds, a technical assistant and startup coach at UNSW's startup incubator, and as an intern at Eppendorf South Pacific.

In my free time, I enjoy testing the limits of my engineering skills through projects such as designing and outfitting my 4WD rig for a journey around Australia.

UNSW Sunswift VII World Record 2022


Our student-built solar race car attained the world record for the "fastest EV over 1000km on a single charge". Sunswift VII features a Carbon Fibre Moncoque Chassis with Aerodynamic drag coefficient of 0.09. It runs 33.9kWh of Li-Ion Samsung cells and 4.6 square meters of solar panels, powering two 9.3kW in-wheel CSIRO axial flux hub motors. During the record attempt, my role was "Chassis and Bodywork Engineer", representing the Chassis and Bodywork department/sub-team.


Link to ABC news article.

Body Work and Chassis Design


During my time on the team, I learnt how to prepare carbon fibre (wet and pre-preg) parts, helping with the bodywork of the Sunswift VII car. I learnt how to analyse and set up composites FEA simulations using ANSYS Composite prep-post and conduct parametric studies varying the carbon layup.


Read my report on Parametric Composite Modelling for a UNSW FEA course.


I also learnt CAD surface modelling for our chassis exterior designs and how to conduct CFD simulations of our modelled geometries in ANSYS Fluent.

Thesis and Air Ducting

Joule Espresso Peter Farrell Cup (PFC) Finalist 2024


My pitch at the PFC Finals Night. As a coffee lover, who couldn't find the perfect barista quality coffee maker to use off-grid whilst driving around Australia, I came up with the idea for "Joule Espresso" - a portable off-grid coffee machine that makes the perfect cup of coffee from anywhere.

The idea is: a temperature and pressure (PID) controlled extraction and steam wand which is powered by a cheap reusuable LPG can. So a $9 LPG can available from any hardware or camping store can make up to 80 "lattes" while you're off-grid. Watch the pitch recording here.

Ultra-compact "Joule" Coffee Maker Concept Animation


Animated rendering showing the desired form-factor and compact design for the Joule Coffee Maker. The portafilter, steam wand and other components store securely in the housing to prevent damage. The machine should be able to be transported easily in the boot of a car, tray of a ute etc.

UG Thesis - Energy Harvesting Suspension


Exploring the use of Energy Harvesting Shock Absorbers (EHSA) to replace traditional viscous dampers in vehicle suspensions, capturing electrical energy passively while driving.

Simulations for the Sunswift VII car estimated maximum power dissipation of 63.38 W per front damper and 60.56W per rear damper. Given an efficiency of 30% for state-of-the-art EHSA, around 18-20 W of power could realistically be harvested. Road tests showed each front damper dissipated about 8.3W at 55 km/h on a smooth course.

Notably, the average power dissipation depends only on vehicle speed, road roughness, and tyre damping, and is unaffected by the vehicle's sprung/unsprung mass or suspension characteristics. Read my complete thesis paper here.

Master's Research - Sentiment Analysis in Financial Tweets


This Master's project explored a variety of classic Reccurrent Neural Network architechtures, including LSTM; and transformer architectures for processing tweets in the Financial-Phrase-Bank Dataset and classifying their sentiment in regards to a financial entity or stock.

The first model uses 300-dimensional GloVe word embeddings for input text, padded to a maximum length of 101 words. A bidirectional LSTM layer (202 hidden units) feeds into a fully connected layer (100 units), with 3 output nodes for classification via Softmax. View the annotated Jupyter Notebook on my Github.

Master's Research - Semantic Image Segmentation on the WildScenes Dataset


This Master's project explores semantic segmentation using DeepLabV3+ with different backbone configurations (ResNet-50 and ResNet-101) to enhance feature extraction capabilities on the WildScenes 2D benchmark dataset. By analysing the impact of extracting features from mid- and high-level residual blocks, the study aimed to determine the optimal balance between capturing fine details and abstract representations. View the annotated Jupyter Notebook on my Github.
